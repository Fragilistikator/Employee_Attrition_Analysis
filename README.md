Employee Attrition Analysis

Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition data using Python, Pandas, Jupyter Notebook, and Tableau to identify factors associated with employee turnover. The goal was to explore workforce trends and determine which factors may contribute to employees leaving an organization.

Objectives

* Analyze overall employee attrition rates.
* Investigate the impact of overtime on employee retention.
* Explore the relationship between job satisfaction and attrition.
* Compare attrition rates across departments.
* Examine differences in income between employees who stayed and employees who left.

Dataset

The dataset contains employee demographic, compensation, satisfaction, and employment information for 1,470 employees.

Key Findings

* The overall employee attrition rate was 16.1%.
* Employees working overtime experienced significantly higher attrition rates (30.5%) than employees who did not work overtime (10.4%).
* Employees with lower job satisfaction showed higher attrition rates than employees with higher satisfaction levels.
* Sales had the highest attrition rate among departments at 20.6%.
* Employees who left the company earned an average monthly income of $4,787 compared to $6,833 for employees who remained.

Dashboard

The Tableau dashboard includes:

* Employee Attrition Rate
* Attrition Rate by Overtime Status
* Attrition Rate by Job Satisfaction
* Attrition Rate by Department
